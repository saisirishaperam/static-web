Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an iconic 17th-century mausoleum located in Agra, India, commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Built entirely of white marble and adorned with intricate carvings and precious stones, it reflects a blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. Surrounded by lush gardens and set beside the Yamuna River, the monument changes color with the sunlight, symbolizing the timeless beauty and love it was built to honor. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it attracts millions of visitors each year as one of the worlds most celebrated wonders.
